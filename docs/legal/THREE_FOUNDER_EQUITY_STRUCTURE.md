# ConTigo Three-Founder Equity Structure

**Purpose:** practical structure for negotiating founder equity between the technical founder, a sales/commercial cofounder, and a data architecture cofounder.

**Status:** founder discussion document, not a final legal agreement.

**Important:** this is not legal advice. A Swiss startup lawyer should convert the chosen structure into the shareholders' agreement, articles of association, IP assignment, reverse-vesting provisions, and any employment or mandate agreements.

## 1. Core Principle

The company is not starting from zero. The product, codebase, architecture, AI pipeline, infrastructure, security work, and deployment preparation were already created by the technical founder before the other founders joined.

That means equity should reflect two different types of value:

| Value type | Who has contributed it | How it should be treated |
|---|---|---|
| **Past value** | Technical founder | Recognised immediately through a larger allocation and partial day-one vesting |
| **Future value** | All founders | Earned through vesting, milestones, customer traction, delivery, and long-term commitment |

The sales and data architecture roles can become highly valuable, but their value is mainly forward-looking unless they are bringing immediate signed customers, funding, enterprise partnerships, proprietary data assets, or already-delivered architecture.

## 2. Recommended Starting Point

Use this as the opening proposal:

| Founder | Role | Equity | Rationale |
|---|---|---:|---|
| **Technical founder** | Product creator, CTO, full-stack/AI/platform owner | **55%** | Built the full app and owns the deepest product/IP knowledge |
| **Sales founder** | Commercial lead, pipeline, customers, pricing, partnerships | **25%** | Owns revenue creation, first customers, pricing, and commercial execution |
| **Data architecture founder** | Data architecture, AI/data governance, enterprise data design | **20%** | Owns enterprise data architecture, AI/data quality, governance, and scalable data systems |
| **Total** |  | **100%** |  |

This is the founder economics target if there is no separate pool at incorporation. It is a generous structure for the two incoming founders because it recognises that sales and data architecture are both strategically important, while still leaving the technical founder with majority control.

### If A 5% Pool Is Needed Immediately

The phrase "55 / 25 / 20 plus 5% pool" adds up to **105%**, so it cannot be issued as a legal cap table without adjustment. There are two clean ways to handle it:

| Option | Technical founder | Sales founder | Data architecture founder | Pool | Total | Notes |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---|
| **No pool at incorporation** | **55%** | **25%** | **20%** | **0%** | **100%** | Use the exact founder split now; create an ESOP/milestone pool later by agreed dilution |
| **Pool carved out now** | **52.25%** | **23.75%** | **19%** | **5%** | **100%** | Preserves the same 55/25/20 relative economics while reserving 5% immediately |

Recommended implementation: use **55 / 25 / 20** as the founder negotiation headline, and either create the 5% pool later when needed or carve it out proportionally at incorporation as **52.25 / 23.75 / 19 / 5**.

### Slide Version: Founder Split Options

| Scenario | Technical founder | Sales founder | Data architecture founder | Pool | Best use |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---|
| **Base founder economics** | **55%** | **25%** | **20%** | **0%** | Cleanest founder split for discussion |
| **Pool carved out now** | **52.25%** | **23.75%** | **19%** | **5%** | Use if advisors/hires/milestones need equity immediately |
| **Commercial-heavy concession** | **50%** | **30%** | **20%** | **0%** | Only if sales brings immediate revenue/customers |
| **Data-heavy concession** | **50%** | **25%** | **25%** | **0%** | Only if data architecture brings exceptional shipped enterprise value |
| **Not recommended** | **33.3%** | **33.3%** | **33.3%** | **0%** | Ignores existing product/IP contribution |

Deck takeaway: start with **55 / 25 / 20**. If a pool is required immediately, use **52.25 / 23.75 / 19 / 5**. Do not present "55 / 25 / 20 plus 5%" because it totals 105%.

## 3. Negotiation Range

The technical founder should normally stay at or above **55% of founder economics** unless another founder brings immediate company-changing value. If a pool is carved out immediately, the technical founder can hold slightly less than 55% on the legal cap table while still preserving the same relative founder economics.

| Scenario | Technical founder | Sales founder | Data architecture founder | When it makes sense |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| **Founder economics target** | **55%** | **25%** | **20%** | Exact founder split before any pool is carved out |
| **With 5% pool carved out now** | **52.25%** | **23.75%** | **19%** | Same relative split, but 5% reserved for ESOP/milestones/advisors |
| **Commercial-heavy concession** | **50%** | **30%** | **20%** | Only if sales founder brings immediate customers, enterprise pipeline, fundraising access, or CEO-level ownership |
| **Data-heavy concession** | **50%** | **25%** | **25%** | Only if data architecture founder brings exceptional enterprise architecture, AI/data assets, compliance leadership, or production delivery |
| **Not recommended** | **33.3%** | **33.3%** | **33.3%** | Equal thirds ignores the already-built product and creates weak founder control |

**Recommended floor:** avoid going below **55% founder economics** for the technical founder unless someone contributes immediate revenue, funding, customer contracts, or capital that materially changes the company. If the legal cap table has a 5% pool from day one, the practical floor becomes **52.25%** for the technical founder.

## 4. Better Structure: Base Equity + Milestone Pool

If both colleagues want more, avoid arguing only about fixed percentages. Create a small milestone pool so extra equity is earned through real outcomes.

### Option A: 5% pool carved out proportionally

| Holder | Equity |
|---|---:|
| Technical founder | **52.25%** |
| Sales founder | **23.75%** |
| Data architecture founder | **19%** |
| Milestone pool | **5%** |
| **Total** | **100%** |

### Option B: No pool at incorporation

| Holder | Equity |
|---|---:|
| Technical founder | **55%** |
| Sales founder | **25%** |
| Data architecture founder | **20%** |
| Milestone pool | **0% now; created later by agreed dilution** |
| **Total** | **100%** |

The milestone pool can be awarded later based on objective achievements. This lets the answer be: "Yes, there is upside, but it must be earned."

## 5. Suggested Milestones

### Sales Founder Milestones

| Milestone | Possible extra equity | Measurement |
|---|---:|---|
| First paying customer signed | 1% | Signed contract and payment received |
| CHF 50k ARR | 1% | Active recurring revenue, not just pipeline |
| CHF 150k ARR | 1.5% | Net revenue retained for at least 3 months |
| CHF 300k ARR | 2% | Verified customer contracts |
| Strategic enterprise partnership or channel | 0.5-1% | Signed partner agreement with measurable opportunity |

### Data Architecture Founder Milestones

| Milestone | Possible extra equity | Measurement |
|---|---:|---|
| Production data architecture plan delivered | 0.5-1% | Approved architecture, diagrams, tradeoffs, implementation roadmap |
| Enterprise-grade data governance implemented | 1% | Tenant isolation, auditability, retention, deletion, data classification |
| AI/RAG quality and evaluation framework implemented | 1% | Automated evals, measurable quality metrics, regression tracking |
| Security/compliance readiness package delivered | 1% | Swiss/EU data handling, customer-facing security pack, DPA support |
| Scalable analytics or data platform shipped | 1-2% | Production usage, measurable customer/internal value |

### Rules For Milestone Equity

- Milestone equity should vest only after the milestone is objectively achieved.
- Pipeline alone should not count as revenue.
- Architecture slides alone should not count as implementation.
- The board or all founders should approve milestone achievement in writing.
- Unused milestone pool should remain available for future hires, advisors, or ESOP.

## 6. Vesting Structure

All founder equity should be subject to reverse vesting, including the technical founder, but the technical founder should receive credit for the already-built product.

### Technical Founder

| Component | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Day-one vested portion | **30-40% of the technical founder's shares** |
| Reason | Product and IP already delivered before company formation |
| Remaining shares | Vest monthly over **36-48 months** |
| Cliff | No cliff, or shorter cliff, because the product already exists |

### Sales Founder

| Component | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Day-one vested portion | **0%** |
| Vesting | **2 years** |
| Cliff | **1 year** |
| Month 12 | **50% of the sales founder's shares vest** if still active |
| Months 13-24 | Remaining **50% vests monthly** |
| Acceleration | Double-trigger only: acquisition plus termination without cause |

### Data Architecture Founder

| Component | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Day-one vested portion | **0%**, unless substantial architecture has already been delivered |
| Vesting | **2 years** |
| Cliff | **1 year** |
| Month 12 | **50% of the data architecture founder's shares vest** if still active |
| Months 13-24 | Remaining **50% vests monthly** |
| Acceleration | Double-trigger only: acquisition plus termination without cause |

## 7. Swiss GmbH Implementation Notes

For a Swiss GmbH, shares are usually issued up front, then controlled through contractual repurchase rights.

Ask counsel to implement:

- **Reverse vesting / repurchase right** for unvested Stammanteile.
- **Good leaver / bad leaver** provisions.
- **IP assignment** from the technical founder to the company.
- **Founder work-product assignment** for all future work by all founders.
- **Vinkulierung** / transfer restrictions in the articles and shareholders' agreement.
- **Right of first refusal** if a founder wants to sell shares.
- **Drag-along and tag-along** rights for future investment or exit.
- **Deadlock process** for major decisions.
- **Confidentiality and non-solicitation** clauses.

Avoid issuing large unconditional equity blocks without vesting. That creates serious risk if someone leaves after a few months.

## 8. Roles And Responsibilities

### Technical Founder

- Own product vision and technical roadmap.
- Own codebase, architecture, AI pipeline, platform reliability, and infrastructure decisions.
- Lead technical hiring and engineering quality.
- Own security-by-design and technical customer due diligence.
- Decide final technical feasibility and delivery estimates.

### Sales Founder

- Own commercial strategy, ICP, pipeline, pricing, demos, proposals, and closing.
- Build repeatable sales process and CRM hygiene.
- Own customer discovery and feedback loop with product.
- Support fundraising narrative and investor conversations.
- Deliver measurable ARR targets.

### Data Architecture Founder

- Own data architecture, governance, RAG/data quality, and enterprise data model.
- Support AI quality, evaluation, metadata extraction reliability, and analytics design.
- Prepare architecture material for enterprise buyers and compliance reviews.
- Improve scalability, data observability, retention, deletion, and tenant isolation.
- Work with the technical founder rather than creating a separate technical authority.

## 9. Decision Rights

The technical founder should keep control over technical/IP decisions while the company is still product-led.

### Ordinary Decisions

Can be handled by the responsible founder within their area:

- Sales process and customer outreach: sales founder.
- Data architecture proposals and data governance: data architecture founder.
- Product architecture, code, infrastructure, security design: technical founder.

### Major Decisions Requiring Founder Approval

- Issuing new shares or options.
- Taking external investment.
- Selling the company or major assets.
- Changing the company purpose.
- Hiring executives or committing to major salaries.
- Taking debt above an agreed threshold.
- Assigning or licensing core IP outside normal customer contracts.
- Changing founder roles, vesting, or compensation.

### Suggested Voting Threshold

For major decisions, require either:

- technical founder approval plus one other founder, or
- 75% shareholder approval.

This prevents two minority founders from forcing a decision that damages the core product/IP owner.

## 10. Compensation

If the company cannot pay market salaries immediately, document salary sacrifice clearly.

| Founder | Early salary approach | Equity implication |
|---|---|---|
| Technical founder | May continue unpaid/underpaid after already building product | Already reflected in majority equity and day-one vesting |
| Sales founder | May be unpaid/underpaid until revenue | Reflected through vesting and possibly commission/milestones |
| Data architecture founder | May be unpaid/underpaid until funding/revenue | Reflected through vesting and delivery milestones |

Potential sales compensation:

- Small commission on first deals can be added instead of increasing fixed equity.
- Commission should not duplicate milestone equity for the same achievement unless explicitly agreed.

## 11. Product Packaging, Pricing, And Revenue Model

ConTigo should be sold first as a **core contract management platform with an AI contract chatbot**. Drafting Studio, advanced drafting workflows, negotiation support, and deeper analytics should be positioned as accessory modules and future expansion, not as the first commercial wedge.

### Slide Version: Product Packaging

| Layer | Positioning | Sell now? | Reason |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Core Contract Management** | Repository, metadata, tags, renewals, obligations, risk, audit trail, contract status | **Yes** | Clear pain, easy buyer understanding, daily operational value |
| **AI Contract Chatbot** | Ask questions over contract text, artifacts, metadata, and contract context | **Yes** | Strong demo value and clear differentiation |
| **Artifact/Insight Generation** | Summaries, clauses, risk, compliance, obligations, timeline, contacts, parties | **Yes, inside core tiers** | Supports contract management and chatbot credibility |
| **Drafting Studio** | Drafting, clause authoring, playbooks, negotiation assistance | **Accessory/future** | Valuable, but can create more legal risk, custom scope, and buyer complexity early |
| **Advanced Enterprise Modules** | SSO, integrations, retention, compliance pack, advanced analytics, custom workflows | **Enterprise/add-on** | Helps expand after the first core contract-management win |

Deck takeaway: lead with **"AI-powered contract management and contract Q&A"**, not with drafting. Drafting is expansion after trust, data, and contract repository adoption are established.

### Core Product Offer

The first commercial offer should be narrow enough to sell quickly but valuable enough to justify annual pricing.

| Buyer pain | Core promise |
|---|---|
| Contracts are scattered across folders, email, SharePoint, or local drives | Central contract repository with searchable metadata |
| Teams miss renewals or obligations | Renewal and obligation visibility |
| Legal/finance/procurement waste time reading PDFs | AI summaries, artifacts, and chatbot Q&A |
| Management lacks contract risk visibility | Risk, compliance, status, and reporting views |
| Enterprise buyers need trust | Audit logs, tenant isolation, security posture, and Swiss/EU data handling |

### Enterprise Core Readiness

Enterprise buyers will not buy only because the chatbot demo is good. They buy when the platform answers security, control, audit, migration, and operational questions convincingly.

| Enterprise buyer question | Required answer | Current status | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|---|
| Can we trust this with confidential contracts? | Security overview, data-flow diagram, encryption, tenant isolation, Swiss/EU data residency, subprocessors, DPA | **Sales collateral needed; security/docs partially exist** | Procurement and security review happen before enterprise rollout |
| Can users authenticate through our company identity provider? | Microsoft Entra ID / OIDC or SAML SSO, role mapping, enforced MFA through customer IdP | **Enterprise roadmap / planned** | Swiss enterprise customers often expect Microsoft 365 identity integration |
| Can we control who sees each contract? | Tenant, role, workspace/team, and contract-level permissions | **Partially available; contract-level access should be hardened** | Contract repositories contain confidential HR, supplier, customer, and legal files |
| Can the workflow run contract operations, not just storage? | Contract owner, status, renewal owner, obligation owner, review state, reminders, escalation | **Partially available; needs productized workflow** | Enterprise buyers need operational contract management, not only search |
| Can we find what matters quickly? | Full-text search, metadata filters, tags, parties, renewal windows, risk, obligations, missing metadata, saved views | **Partially available; should become demo-critical** | Search and filtering are the everyday value of contract management |
| Can we trust chatbot answers? | Source citations, exact clause snippets, answer limitations, distinction between contract text and AI interpretation | **Partially available; needs stronger source evidence UX** | Legal and procurement teams need traceability, not black-box answers |
| Can we migrate existing contracts? | Bulk upload, CSV metadata import, SharePoint/OneDrive roadmap, duplicate detection, failed import report | **Architecture documented; implementation/productization needed** | Migration is how enterprise customers get to first value |
| Can management see portfolio risk? | Dashboards for renewals, high-risk contracts, missing metadata, obligations by owner, processing status | **Partially available; reporting should be prioritized** | Buyers need proof of value and management visibility |
| Can admins audit and control usage? | User/role management, audit log viewer/export, data retention, tenant export/delete, usage and plan controls | **Partially available; enterprise admin polish needed** | Admin controls justify Enterprise pricing |

Use paid pilots to create early cash and prove value without giving away months of work.

## 12. How Each Person Is Rewarded

Each founder should be rewarded through a mix of ownership, vesting, role-based compensation, milestone upside, profit distributions, and exit proceeds. The key is to avoid rewarding only promises. Equity should reward long-term company building; cash/commission should reward near-term execution.

### Reward Overview

| Founder | Main reward | Additional reward | What they must deliver |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Technical founder** | Majority equity and partial day-one vesting | CTO salary when cash allows, dividends, exit proceeds | Product, IP, AI system, platform reliability, technical roadmap, security, delivery |
| **Sales founder** | Cofounder equity vesting over time | Sales commission or milestone equity tied to ARR | Pipeline, paid pilots, signed customers, pricing discipline, expansion revenue |
| **Data architecture founder** | Cofounder equity vesting over time | Milestone equity tied to shipped architecture/data outcomes | Data architecture, AI quality, governance, enterprise data trust, customer technical confidence |
| **Future pool recipients** | Small equity grants or phantom shares | Performance bonuses where appropriate | Advisors, key hires, or contributors who create measurable value |

### Technical Founder Reward

The technical founder is rewarded for both past and future contribution.

| Reward component | Recommendation | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Equity | **55% founder economics** or **52.25% if a 5% pool is carved out immediately** | Recognises that the product and IP already exist because of the technical founder |
| Day-one vesting | **30-40% of technical founder shares vested immediately** | Prior work has already been delivered |
| Remaining vesting | Monthly over **36-48 months** | Keeps long-term commitment aligned |
| Salary | Start low or deferred if cash is tight; move toward market CTO salary after funding/revenue | Avoids draining cash too early while recognising ongoing full-time work |
| Bonus | Optional product/reliability bonus after revenue milestones | Useful only once cash flow is stable |
| Dividends/exit | Paid according to shareholding | Main long-term upside |

Practical rule: the technical founder should not also need to "earn" the already-built product again. They should earn future vesting by continuing to lead product and technology.

### Sales Founder Reward

The sales founder should be rewarded heavily when revenue becomes real, not just when pipeline exists.

| Reward component | Recommendation | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Equity | **25% founder economics** or **23.75% if a 5% pool is carved out immediately** | Meaningful cofounder ownership for owning revenue |
| Day-one vesting | **0%** | Commercial value is mostly future-facing |
| Vesting | **2 years with 1-year cliff** | Protects the company if sales does not materialise |
| Commission | Optional **5-10% of first-year ARR** on founder-led first deals, or lower if equity is already high | Rewards closed revenue without increasing permanent equity |
| Milestone upside | Possible pool grants for CHF 50k, CHF 150k, CHF 300k ARR | Rewards measurable outcomes |
| Salary | Modest base once funding/revenue allows | Keeps incentives focused on revenue early |
| Dividends/exit | Paid according to vested shareholding | Long-term upside follows company value |

Commission and milestone equity should not double-count the same outcome unless all founders explicitly agree. For example, if the sales founder receives extra equity for CHF 150k ARR, the commission on that ARR should be modest.

### Data Architecture Founder Reward

The data architecture founder should be rewarded for shipped enterprise value, not just architecture opinions or diagrams.

| Reward component | Recommendation | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Equity | **20% founder economics** or **19% if a 5% pool is carved out immediately** | Strong cofounder ownership for data/AI architecture importance |
| Day-one vesting | **0%**, unless substantial data architecture was already delivered before signing | Most value is future-facing |
| Vesting | **2 years with 1-year cliff** | Protects the company if contribution does not continue |
| Milestone upside | Pool grants for shipped governance, AI quality, evaluation, scalability, security/compliance support | Rewards concrete delivery |
| Salary | Modest base once funding/revenue allows | Recognises ongoing technical/data work |
| Bonus | Optional delivery bonus for enterprise readiness or security/compliance milestones | Useful if cash exists and milestones are customer-relevant |
| Dividends/exit | Paid according to vested shareholding | Long-term upside follows company value |

Practical rule: data architecture rewards should be tied to production impact, customer trust, AI/data quality, and reduced enterprise sales friction.

### Pool Rewards

The 5% pool should be used carefully. It should not be treated as extra founder equity unless earned.

| Recipient type | Typical grant | Vesting / condition |
|---|---:|---|
| Strategic advisor | 0.25-1% | 12-24 months, clear advisory deliverables |
| First senior hire | 0.5-2% | 4-year vesting with 1-year cliff |
| Sales/data milestone grant | 0.5-2% | Only after objective milestone achievement |
| Board/advisory expert | 0.25-0.75% | Time-bound and deliverable-based |

Unused pool should remain with the company or be available for future hiring. It should not automatically be split between founders.

### Reward Timing

| Stage | Technical founder | Sales founder | Data architecture founder |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Incorporation** | Equity issued; 30-40% of own shares vested for prior IP | Equity issued but unvested | Equity issued but unvested |
| **First 12 months** | Continues vesting; low/deferred salary if needed | Cliff period; commission only on closed revenue if agreed | Cliff period; milestone recognition only for shipped work |
| **Month 12** | Continues monthly vesting | 50% of sales founder shares vest if still active | 50% of data architecture founder shares vest if still active |
| **Months 13-24** | Continues monthly vesting | Remaining 50% vests monthly | Remaining 50% vests monthly |
| **After first revenue** | CTO salary can begin increasing | Base/commission structure can begin | Base or project compensation can begin |
| **After funding or stable ARR** | Move toward market salary | Move toward sales leadership salary plus targets | Move toward data/architecture leadership salary |
| **Profit or exit** | Paid by vested ownership | Paid by vested ownership | Paid by vested ownership |

### Simple Rule For Fairness

- **Equity** rewards long-term ownership and risk.
- **Vesting** rewards staying and building.
- **Commission** rewards closed revenue.
- **Milestones** reward measurable company value.
- **Salary** rewards ongoing work once the company can afford it.
- **Dividends/exit proceeds** reward actual ownership when the company creates value.

## 13. Example Reward Scenarios

This section shows how the reward structure works in practice. The exact numbers should be reviewed with a Swiss lawyer and tax advisor, but the logic is useful for founder alignment.

### Scenario A: Incorporation With No Pool Yet

| Founder | Equity | Vesting position | Immediate reward |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Technical founder | **55%** | 30-40% of own shares vested immediately; rest vests monthly | Recognition for already-built product/IP plus continued CTO ownership |
| Sales founder | **25%** | 0% vested until 1-year cliff; fully vested after 2 years | Meaningful upside, earned through staying and closing revenue |
| Data architecture founder | **20%** | 0% vested until 1-year cliff; fully vested after 2 years | Meaningful upside, earned through staying and shipping data/AI value |

This is the simplest structure. The company can create a 5% pool later by proportional dilution when it actually needs grants for hires, advisors, or milestones.

### Scenario B: Incorporation With 5% Pool Immediately

| Holder | Equity | Reward logic |
|---|---:|---|
| Technical founder | **52.25%** | Keeps the same relative 55% founder economics after pool carveout |
| Sales founder | **23.75%** | Keeps the same relative 25% founder economics after pool carveout |
| Data architecture founder | **19%** | Keeps the same relative 20% founder economics after pool carveout |
| Pool | **5%** | Reserved for milestone grants, advisors, hires, or future ESOP |

This is cleaner if the founders already know they need advisor or milestone equity from day one.

### Scenario C: First CHF 100k ARR

| Founder | Reward |
|---|---|
| Technical founder | Product/CTO credibility increases; salary can start moving up if cash allows; equity value increases |
| Sales founder | Possible commission on first-year ARR if agreed; milestone credit for closed revenue; stronger case for future pool grant |
| Data architecture founder | Rewarded if enterprise data/security/AI quality work materially helped close the customer |

Important: pipeline does not count. The reward should be based on signed contracts and received payment.

### Scenario D: First Enterprise Customer

| Founder | Reward |
|---|---|
| Technical founder | Recognised for platform readiness, security answers, technical due diligence, and implementation feasibility |
| Sales founder | Recognised for commercial ownership, negotiation, procurement navigation, and closing |
| Data architecture founder | Recognised if data governance, AI quality, architecture, security, or compliance support helped pass enterprise review |

If all three materially contribute, the company should reward each through role-specific salary progression or milestone recognition rather than changing the base cap table.

### Scenario E: Profit Distributions

If the company becomes profitable and chooses to distribute dividends, distributions should follow vested ownership and Swiss tax/legal rules.

Example with **55 / 25 / 20** and CHF 100,000 distributable profit:

| Founder | Share | Example dividend |
|---|---:|---:|
| Technical founder | 55% | CHF 55,000 |
| Sales founder | 25% | CHF 25,000 |
| Data architecture founder | 20% | CHF 20,000 |

In early stages, it is usually better to reinvest profit into product, sales, infrastructure, and hiring rather than distribute dividends.

### Scenario F: Exit Proceeds

If the company is sold, proceeds follow vested ownership after investor preferences, debts, transaction costs, taxes, and legal obligations.

Example with **55 / 25 / 20** and CHF 10,000,000 net founder proceeds:

| Founder | Share | Example proceeds |
|---|---:|---:|
| Technical founder | 55% | CHF 5,500,000 |
| Sales founder | 25% | CHF 2,500,000 |
| Data architecture founder | 20% | CHF 2,000,000 |

Example with **52.25 / 23.75 / 19 / 5** and CHF 10,000,000 net proceeds:

| Holder | Share | Example proceeds |
|---|---:|---:|
| Technical founder | 52.25% | CHF 5,225,000 |
| Sales founder | 23.75% | CHF 2,375,000 |
| Data architecture founder | 19% | CHF 1,900,000 |
| Pool / option holders | 5% | CHF 500,000 |

These examples are simple illustrations. Real exit proceeds depend on investor rights, vesting, leaver status, taxes, and the final shareholders' agreement.

## 15. Talk Track For The Conversation

Use calm, non-defensive language:

> I want this to feel fair, and I also want us to be precise. My proposal is 55% for me, 25% for sales, and 20% for data architecture. That recognises the fact that I already built the app and still gives both of you meaningful cofounder ownership. If we want a 5% pool from day one, we need to carve it out proportionally because 55 plus 25 plus 20 plus 5 equals 105%.

If they ask for equal thirds:

> Equal thirds would make sense if we were starting from an idea. We are not. There is already a working product and IP. I am open to meaningful cofounder equity, but not to ignoring the value already created.

If sales asks for more:

> I agree that revenue is critical. That is exactly why I am open to milestone equity tied to signed customers and ARR. I do not want to grant future sales value before it exists, but I do want to reward it strongly when it happens.

If data architecture asks for more:

> I agree the data architecture role can become very important, especially for enterprise trust and AI quality. The right way to recognise that is through delivery milestones: governance, evaluation, architecture, compliance, and production impact.

If they ask how the company will make money:

> We should sell core contract management plus the AI contract chatbot first. The first commercial package should be annual SaaS, supported by paid pilots, onboarding fees, and usage-based AI/OCR add-ons. Drafting Studio is a future expansion module, not the first sales wedge.

## 16. Red Lines

Do not sign a founders' agreement that lacks these protections:

- No vesting.
- No IP assignment to the company.
- No recognition of the technical founder's pre-existing work.
- Equal thirds without a strong reason.
- No leaver provisions.
- No repurchase right for unvested shares.
- No role clarity.
- No decision rights for major IP/product decisions.
- No written milestone criteria for extra equity.

## 17. Practical Recommendation

The best structure is:

| Holder | Equity |
|---|---:|
| Technical founder | **55%** |
| Sales founder | **25%** |
| Data architecture founder | **20%** |

With:

- technical founder partially vested on day one for prior product/IP creation;
- sales and data architecture founders on 2-year vesting with 1-year cliff;
- a 5% pool created later or carved out proportionally as **52.25 / 23.75 / 19 / 5**;
- all future work assigned to the company;
- Swiss lawyer review before signing.

If the group wants the 5% pool immediately, use:

| Holder | Equity |
|---|---:|
| Technical founder | **52.25%** |
| Sales founder | **23.75%** |
| Data architecture founder | **19%** |
| Pool | **5%** |

This preserves the requested 55 / 25 / 20 economics while keeping the legal cap table at exactly 100%.

The main revenue approach should be:

- sell core contract management plus AI contract chatbot as annual SaaS;
- target CHF 36k-60k/year Business plans as the first serious commercial wedge;
- run CHF 7.5k-15k paid pilots instead of free pilots;
- charge onboarding and migration fees;
- add usage-based pricing for AI/OCR-heavy usage;
- keep Drafting Studio as accessory/future expansion;
- expand from one department to legal, finance, procurement, and management.

Do not give away additional equity beyond this unless someone brings immediate signed revenue, meaningful capital, or a customer/funding asset that changes the company from day one.
