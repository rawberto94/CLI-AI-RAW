|---|---|---|---|
| **Technical Founder** | **70%** | CHF 14,000 | Built the entire product, IP creator, ongoing CTO |
| **Co-Founder** | **25%** | CHF 5,000 | Business development, sales, fundraising |
| **ESOP Pool** | **5%** | CHF 1,000 | Reserved for future key hires |

#### Option B: Co-Founder Joins with Significant Capital/Network

| Founder | Equity (Stammanteile) | CHF Capital | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Technical Founder** | **60%** | CHF 12,000 | Built the entire product, IP creator, ongoing CTO |
| **Co-Founder** | **30%** | CHF 6,000 | Brings capital (>CHF 50K), enterprise sales network, or domain expertise |
| **ESOP Pool** | **10%** | CHF 2,000 | Larger pool if aggressive hiring planned |
| **Total** | **100%** | **CHF 20,000** | |

### 2.2 Equity Valuation Basis

The equity split accounts for:

| Factor | Technical Founder Credit |
|---|---|
| **Pre-existing IP** | 240,000+ lines of production code, AI architecture, 10+ documentation files |
| **Time invested** | ~12–24 months FTE equivalent |
| **Opportunity cost** | Forgone salary of CHF 150,000–300,000 (Swiss senior engineer market rate) |
| **Risk taken** | Built product without compensation or guarantee |
| **Ongoing contribution** | Will continue as full-time CTO and lead architect |
| **Domain knowledge** | Deep expertise in AI/ML, contract analysis, Swiss compliance |

The Co-Founder's equity accounts for:

| Factor | Co-Founder Credit |
|---|---|
| **Future contribution** | Sales, operations, fundraising — value to be proven |
| **Capital contribution** | Share capital payment + any additional investment |
| **Network & relationships** | Industry connections, potential customer intros |
| **Commitment** | Full-time dedication going forward |

> **Key principle:** The Technical Founder's equity is partially earned from Day 0 (reflecting past contribution). The Co-Founder's equity is entirely forward-looking and subject to vesting.

### 2.3 Anti-Dilution Protections (Technical Founder)

To protect the Technical Founder's position through future funding rounds:

1. **Minimum ownership threshold:** Technical Founder should not fall below **35%** ownership before Series A without written consent.
2. **Pro-rata rights:** Both founders have the right to participate pro-rata in future funding rounds.
3. **Founder preference on liquidation:** In a liquidation event, founders receive their capital contributions back before any distribution (standard GmbH/Swiss practice).

### 2.4 Phantom Equity / Sweat Equity Valuation

To formalise the Technical Founder's pre-existing contribution for accounting and tax purposes:

| Method | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| **Cost approach** (hours × rate) | 2,000 hours × CHF 150/hr | CHF 300,000 |
| **Market approach** (replacement cost) | 3 senior engineers × 12 months × CHF 14,000/month | CHF 504,000 |
| **Income approach** (DCF of Year 1 revenue attributed to existing product) | CHF 440,000 ARR × 0.7 probability × 3x multiple | CHF 924,000 |

> **Recommended valuation for IP assignment:** CHF 300,000–500,000. This amount is the "consideration" for the IP transfer and supports the 70% equity allocation. Have this documented by the Treuhänder and confirmed in the Articles of Association.

## 3. Vesting Schedule

### 3.1 Technical Founder Vesting

Given that the Technical Founder has **already created the product**, a modified vesting schedule applies:

| Period | Vested | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **Day 0 (incorporation)** | **40%** of Technical Founder's shares | Reflects pre-existing IP contribution |
| **Months 1–36** | Remaining **60%** vests monthly (1.67%/month) | Ensures continued commitment |
| **Cliff** | None (Day 0 vesting replaces cliff) | IP already delivered |
| **Acceleration** | **Double-trigger:** (1) Change of control AND (2) termination without cause within 12 months post-acquisition → 100% acceleration. Single-trigger (change of control alone) → 50% of unvested shares accelerate. Termination without cause (no CoC) → 100% acceleration with 6-month post-termination exercise window |

**Example (70% total equity):**
- Day 0: 28% vested immediately (40% of 70%)
- Month 1–36: 42% vests over 36 months (~1.17% per month)
- By Month 36: 70% fully vested

### 3.2 Co-Founder Vesting

Standard startup vesting applies — **the Co-Founder has no pre-existing contribution to accelerate:**

| Period | Vested | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **Month 0–12** | **0%** (cliff) | Must prove commitment for 12 months |
| **Month 12** | **25%** cliff vests | Standard 1-year cliff |
| **Months 13–48** | Remaining **75%** vests monthly (2.08%/month) | 4-year total vesting |
| **Acceleration** | 50% upon change of control; 100% if terminated within 12 months post-acquisition | Double-trigger |

**Example (25% total equity):**
- Month 0–11: 0% vested
- Month 12: 6.25% vested (cliff)
- Month 13–48: 18.75% vests over 36 months (~0.52% per month)
- By Month 48: 25% fully vested

### 3.3 Reverse Vesting Mechanism (Swiss Implementation)

Under Swiss law, GmbH shares are typically fully issued at incorporation. Vesting is implemented via a **Share Restriction Agreement (Vinkulierung)**:

1. All shares are issued at incorporation.
2. Unvested shares are subject to a **repurchase right** (Rückkaufsrecht) at nominal value.
3. Upon departure before full vesting, the company (or remaining founders) may repurchase unvested shares at CHF 1/share (nominal value).
4. This must be documented in the **Articles of Association** (Statuten) and the **Founders' Agreement**.
5. **Vesting pause:** If a founder takes any authorised leave of absence exceeding **30 consecutive calendar days** (other than medical leave with certificate), the vesting clock is **paused** for the duration of the absence and resumes upon return. Medical leave up to 6 months does NOT pause vesting.
6. **Acceleration ceiling:** No accelerated vesting event may result in the Co-Founder having more vested shares than the Technical Founder at any point in time.

## 4. IP Assignment

### 4.1 Pre-Existing IP Assignment

> **This is the most critical clause for the Technical Founder's protection and for investor due diligence.**

The Technical Founder agrees to assign all pre-existing intellectual property related to ConTigo to the GmbH, **subject to the following conditions:**

| Condition | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Assignment scope** | All source code, designs, architecture documents, AI prompts, training data, documentation created for the ConTigo platform |
| **Effective upon** | GmbH incorporation AND execution of this agreement |
| **Consideration** | Technical Founder's equity allocation (70% or 60% per Section 2) constitutes full consideration for the IP assignment |
| **Retained rights** | Technical Founder retains **no** licence to use ConTigo IP outside the company |
| **Moral rights** | Technical Founder retains authorship attribution (Swiss URG Art. 9) |
| **Warranty** | Technical Founder warrants that the IP is original work, not encumbered by third-party claims, and does not infringe any third-party IP |
| **Open source** | Technical Founder warrants that all open-source dependencies are used under permissive licences (MIT, Apache 2.0) with no GPL contamination of proprietary code |

### 4.2 Future IP

All IP created by any founder during their engagement with the company belongs to the company. This includes:

- Code, algorithms, models, prompts
- Business plans, strategies, customer lists
- Trade secrets, know-how
- Marketing materials, content

This is consistent with Swiss OR Art. 332 (employer ownership of employee inventions created in the course of employment).

### 4.3 IP Reversion on Bad Leaver Termination

If the **Co-Founder** departs as a Bad Leaver (Section 11):
- All IP created by the Co-Founder during their engagement reverts to (or remains with) the company.
- No licence back is granted.

If the **Technical Founder** is forced out without cause before full vesting:
- The Technical Founder retains their vested shares but the IP remains with the company.
- The Technical Founder receives a **12-month consulting retainer** at CHF [10,000–15,000]/month to ensure transition.

## 6. Decision-Making & Governance

### 6.1 Ordinary Business Decisions

- Day-to-day decisions within each founder's domain of authority (Section 5) are made **independently**.
- Both founders commit to weekly 1:1 alignment meetings.

### 6.2 Material Decisions (Require Unanimous Consent)

The following decisions require **unanimous founder approval**:

1. Raising capital or issuing new shares
2. Taking on debt exceeding CHF 10,000
3. Any single expenditure exceeding CHF 20,000
4. Hiring or terminating any employee with salary >CHF 100,000
5. Entering into contracts with commitments exceeding CHF 50,000
6. Changing the company's Articles of Association
7. Pivoting the core product direction
8. Selling, licensing, or encumbering the company's IP
9. Acquiring or merging with another company
10. Distributing profits / dividends

### 6.3 Gesellschafterversammlung (Shareholder Meeting)

- Annual meeting (ordentliche Gesellschafterversammlung) per OR Art. 805
- Extraordinary meetings called with 10 days written notice
- Quorum: Both founders present (or represented by proxy)
- Voting: Per share capital ratio (e.g., 70/25 or 60/30)

### 6.4 Geschäftsführung (Management)

- Both founders are registered as Geschäftsführer (managing directors) in the Handelsregister
- Both have **collective signature authority** (Kollektivunterschrift zu zweien) for banking and material contracts
- Individual signature authority (Einzelunterschrift) for amounts up to CHF 10,000

### 6.5 Written Resolutions (Zirkularbeschlüsse)

Resolutions of the Gesellschafterversammlung may be passed in writing (without a meeting) per OR Art. 805 para. 4, provided:
- The resolution is circulated in writing to all founders
- All founders sign the same text
- No founder requests an oral discussion within 5 business days

### 6.6 Expense & Financial Oversight

- Each founder’s expenses are reviewed by the other founder on a **monthly basis**
- Both founders have **read-only access** to all company bank accounts, credit cards, and accounting systems at all times
- Any expense or payment that appears irregular may be flagged for immediate review; the spending founder must provide documentation within **5 business days**
- The company’s Treuhand (Treuhänder / accountant) produces a **monthly financial package** available to both founders by the 15th of the following month
- Annual financial statements are reviewed by both founders and, if required, the Revisionsstellle

### 7.6 Commercial Co-Founder — Performance-Based Commission

#### 7.6.4 Commission Terms & Conditions

1. **Payment timing:** Commissions are calculated monthly and paid with the following month's salary.
2. **Cap:** Total annual commission is **uncapped** in Year 1–2 to maximise growth incentive. From Year 3, the Gesellschafterversammlung may introduce a cap (e.g., 2× base salary).
3. **Attribution:** A deal is attributed to the Commercial Co-Founder if they are the **primary relationship owner** in the CRM at time of contract signature. Inbound / self-service sign-ups (no sales touch) are excluded.
4. **Clawback:** If a client churns within **6 months** of signing, the commission is clawed back pro-rata (remaining months / 12 × commission amount). The clawback is deducted from the next commission payment.
5. **Minimum guarantee (pre-seed only):** During the first 6 months post-funding, if commission earnings fall below CHF 1,500/month, the company tops up to that floor. This guarantee ends after Month 6.
6. **Reporting:** The Commercial Co-Founder provides a monthly pipeline and commission report. Both founders have read access to the CRM and billing system for full transparency.
7. **Tax treatment:** Commissions are paid as part of gross salary (subject to AHV/IV/EO/ALV deductions). The company handles withholding and Sozialversicherungen as for any Swiss employment income.
8. **Transition to sales team:** When the company hires dedicated sales representatives, the Commercial Co-Founder transitions to a **management override** of 2–3% on deals closed by their direct reports, replacing the personal commission on those accounts.

### 8.2 Non-Compete Clause

During engagement and for **18 months** after departure:

- Geographic scope: Switzerland, DACH, and EU.
- This clause is explicitly agreed to be enforceable under Swiss OR Art. 340 ff. (post-termination non-compete), with the company paying **50% of last annual salary** as compensation during the restriction period (as required by Swiss case law for enforceability).
- **Bad Leaver exception:** If a founder departs as a Bad Leaver (Section 11.2) or Aggravated Bad Leaver (Section 11.2.1), the non-compete compensation is **reduced to 25%** of last annual salary. Swiss courts have upheld reduced compensation where the departing party has breached their obligations (BGE 130 III 353).
- **Forfeiture of non-compete compensation:** If the departing founder breaches the non-compete clause, all future compensation payments are immediately forfeited, and the company may seek injunctive relief and damages.

### 8.3 Anti-Poaching

For **24 months** after departure, neither founder may:
- **Directly or indirectly recruit, solicit, or hire** any employee, contractor, or consultant of the company
- **Encourage or facilitate** any employee to resign from the company
- Offer employment or engagement to anyone who was employed by the company within the 6 months preceding the founder's departure
- This applies whether the departing founder acts personally, through a new employer, or through any intermediary

> **Penalty (Konventionalstrafe):** Breach of the anti-poaching clause triggers a contractual penalty of **CHF 50,000 per person poached**, in addition to any damages. The penalty is payable without proof of damage (OR Art. 160 ff.).

### 8.4 Non-Solicitation of Clients

For **24 months** after departure, neither founder may:
- Solicit or attempt to do business with any customer or prospect of the company
- Redirect any business opportunity away from the company
- Use any customer list, contact information, or pipeline data obtained during their engagement

> **Penalty (Konventionalstrafe):** Breach of client non-solicitation triggers a contractual penalty of **CHF 100,000 per client solicited**, in addition to any damages (OR Art. 160 ff.).

### 8.5 Company Resources & Data

- All company data, documents, code, credentials, and physical assets must be returned or securely deleted upon departure (see Section 11.5 for handover obligations)

## 9. Exit & Transfer Provisions

### 9.1 Lock-Up Period

Neither founder may **sell, transfer, pledge, or encumber** any shares for:
- **24 months** from the date of GmbH incorporation (absolute lock-up)
- **12 months** following any priced funding round (post-funding lock-up)

Exceptions to lock-up:
- Transfers to a wholly-owned personal holding company (Section 9.5)
- Forced transfers under Bad Leaver provisions (Section 11)
- Transfers upon death (to estate, subject to ROFR)

### 9.2 Right of First Refusal (ROFR)

If any founder wishes to sell their shares after the lock-up period:
1. They must first offer shares to the other founder(s) at the proposed price.
2. Remaining founders have **60 days** to exercise ROFR.
3. If not exercised, shares may be sold to the third party at the same or higher price.
4. This must be codified in the Articles of Association (Vinkulierung per OR Art. 786).
5. If the selling founder does not have a bona fide third-party offer, the price is determined by an **independent valuation** (Practitioner Method per Kreisschreiben Nr. 28, or a recent funding round valuation if <12 months old, whichever is higher).

### 9.3 Tag-Along Rights

If any founder sells >50% of their shares to a third party, the other founder(s) have the right to sell their shares on **the same terms and conditions** (price per share, representations, warranties, and indemnities).

### 9.4 Drag-Along Rights

If founders holding >75% of equity agree to a sale of the company, they may compel the remaining founders to sell on the same terms, **subject to:**
- **Pre-Series A:** Drag-along requires **both founders' consent** (unanimous), regardless of equity percentage
- **Post-Series A:** Drag-along may be triggered by shareholders holding >75% of equity (including investors), subject to board approval
- The drag-along price must be at least **Fair Market Value** as determined by an independent valuation or the price offered by the acquirer, whichever is higher
- The dragged founder receives the **same price per share** as the triggering founder(s)

### 9.5 Permitted Transfers

Shares may be transferred without ROFR restrictions to:
- A holding company **wholly owned** by the founder (the founder must remain 100% owner of the holding; any change in holding ownership requires ROFR notification)
- A trust for the benefit of the founder's family (with prior written consent of the other founder)
- (With unanimous consent) An incoming strategic co-founder

### 9.6 Liquidation Preference

In a liquidation or sale event:
1. Debts and obligations are settled first
2. Founders receive back their capital contributions (Stammeinlage)
3. Remaining proceeds distributed pro-rata per equity
4. If investor preferred shares exist, those terms supersede

### 9.7 Change of Control Definition

A **“Change of Control”** means any of the following:
- Any person or group acquires >50% of the company’s share capital or voting rights
- A merger, consolidation, or reorganisation where existing shareholders hold <50% of the surviving entity
- Sale of all or substantially all company assets (>80% by value)
- An IPO (listing on a recognised exchange)
- A change in the composition of the Geschäftsführung such that neither original founder is a managing director

Change of Control triggers:
1. Acceleration provisions per Section 3 (vesting)
2. ESOP acceleration per Section 13.2
3. Mandatory review of all founder agreements within 30 days
4. Domain reversion assessment per Section 4.5

## 10. Deadlock Resolution

If the founders cannot reach agreement on a material decision (Section 6.2) after 30 days:

### Step 1: Mediation (Days 1–30)
- Engage a neutral Swiss mediator (e.g., Swiss Chambers' Arbitration Institution)
- Both founders participate in good faith
- Cost shared equally

### Step 2: Advisory Board Input (Days 30–60)
- If an advisory board exists, seek a non-binding recommendation
- Both founders present their position

### Step 3: Swiss Arbitration (Days 60+)
- Binding arbitration under the Swiss Rules of International Arbitration (Swiss Chambers)
- Seat of arbitration: Zurich
- Language: English (or German by agreement)
- Decision is final and binding

### Step 4: Shotgun Clause (Last Resort)
If arbitration fails or is disproportionate:
- Either founder may invoke a **buy-sell (shotgun) clause**: Founder A offers to buy Founder B's shares at a stated price. Founder B must either accept or buy Founder A's shares at the same price.
- **Cooling-off period:** The receiving founder has **60 days** (not less) to decide. During this period, both founders continue full-time duties.
- **Financing transparency:** Before the shotgun can be invoked, both founders must disclose their **personal financial capacity** to fund the purchase (bank statement or financing commitment letter). A founder who invokes the shotgun without demonstrable ability to fund the purchase forfeits the right to invoke it.
- **Minimum price floor:** The shotgun price per share must be at least **1.5× the nominal value** or the last independent valuation, whichever is higher. This prevents predatory low-ball offers.
- **Independent valuation option:** The receiving founder may, within 10 days of receiving the shotgun notice, request an **independent valuation** (at shared cost). The valuation must be completed within 30 days and becomes the floor price.
- This prevents permanent deadlock while ensuring fair valuation and protecting the financially weaker founder.

## 11. Dissolution & Bad Leaver

### 11.1 Good Leaver Scenarios

A founder is a **Good Leaver** if they depart due to:
- Mutual agreement
- Death or permanent disability (>6 months)
- Constructive dismissal (material breach by the other founder)

**Good Leaver treatment:**
- Retains all vested shares
- Unvested shares are repurchased at **Fair Market Value** (FMV), determined by an independent Swiss auditor (Revisionsstelle)
- 6-month transition support (reasonable)

### 11.2 Bad Leaver Scenarios

A founder is a **Bad Leaver** if they:
- Resign without cause before 24 months (Co-Founder) or 18 months (Technical Founder)
- Are terminated for cause (fraud, gross negligence, breach of non-compete, criminal conviction)
- Materially breach this agreement and fail to cure within 30 days of notice
- **Abandon the company** — defined as: (a) failing to perform duties for **15 consecutive business days** without written authorisation or medical certificate, or (b) becoming unreachable for **10 consecutive business days** (no response to email, phone, or registered letter)
- Fail to serve the mandatory **notice period** (Section 11.4) or refuse to complete the **handover obligations** (Section 11.5)

**Bad Leaver treatment:**
- **All unvested shares** are repurchased at **nominal value** (CHF 1/share)
- **50% of vested shares** are repurchased at nominal value
- Remaining 50% of vested shares are repurchased at the **lower of** nominal value or FMV
- Non-compete and non-solicitation clauses remain in full effect
- No transition compensation
- All outstanding commissions (if applicable) are **forfeited**

#### 11.2.1 Aggravated Bad Leaver (Abandonment)

If a founder is classified as a Bad Leaver specifically due to **abandonment** (as defined above) or **refusal to complete handover**, a harsher regime applies:

- **100% of unvested shares** repurchased at nominal value (same as standard Bad Leaver)
- **75% of vested shares** repurchased at nominal value (instead of 50%)
- Remaining 25% of vested shares repurchased at the **lower of** nominal value or FMV
- The departing founder is **liable for direct damages** caused by the abandonment (e.g., lost client relationships, recruitment costs for replacement), capped at CHF 200,000 or the value of their remaining shares, whichever is lower
- If the departing founder is the **Commercial Co-Founder**, all pipeline, CRM data, client contacts, and sales materials remain company property. No commission payments are owed for any unsigned pipeline.

> **Rationale:** A founder who simply walks away causes disproportionate harm compared to one who negotiates a departure. The harsher treatment reflects this and discourages abandonment.

### 11.3 Quiet Quitting / Persistent Underperformance

A founder who **remains nominally active but materially fails to contribute** is subject to a structured performance process:

| Step | Trigger | Timeline | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Verbal warning** | 1 quarter significantly below KPIs (Section 5.3) | Immediate | Documented discussion, agreed improvement plan |
| **2. Written warning** | 2 consecutive quarters below KPIs **or** repeated failure to attend agreed meetings/commitments | Within 14 days of second quarter close | Formal letter citing specific deficiencies; 90-day improvement period |
| **3. Performance review** | End of 90-day improvement period | Day 90 | Joint review (or advisory board review if deadlock) |
| **4. Reclassification** | No meaningful improvement after 90-day period | Day 91+ | Gesellschafterversammlung may vote to reclassify the underperforming founder as a **Bad Leaver** (requires Advisory Board confirmation if available) |

**Additional protections against passive departure:**
- Each founder must log a **minimum of 35 billable/productive hours per week** (tracked via time-logging tool or written weekly summary)
- Any absence exceeding **5 consecutive business days** requires written notice to the other founder
- A founder who reduces their commitment below 50% of full-time for **4 consecutive weeks** without written agreement is deemed to have **constructively resigned** and is treated as a Bad Leaver

> **Swiss law note:** Under OR Art. 337d, if an employee unjustifiably refuses to start work or leaves without notice, the employer is entitled to compensation equal to ¼ of monthly salary. While founders are Geschäftsführer, not employees, this principle is incorporated contractually via the abandonment clause above.

### 11.4 Mandatory Notice Period

Any founder wishing to depart voluntarily must provide:

| Founder | Minimum Notice | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **Technical Founder** | **6 months** written notice | Time to hire senior engineering replacement and transfer critical systems knowledge |
| **Commercial Co-Founder** | **3 months** written notice | Time to transition client relationships and pipeline |

- Notice must be delivered by **registered letter** (Einschreiben) to the other founder and to the company's registered address.
- During the notice period, the departing founder must continue full-time duties and cooperate with the handover (Section 11.5).
- The company may, at its sole discretion, place the departing founder on **garden leave** (Freistellung) during part or all of the notice period, during which salary continues but the founder may not contact clients, partners, or employees on company matters.
- Failure to serve the full notice period triggers **Bad Leaver** classification.

### 11.5 Mandatory Handover Obligations

Before or during the notice period, the departing founder must complete:

#### Technical Founder Handover
| Item | Deliverable |
|---|---|
| **System access** | Full credentials, API keys, infrastructure access transferred to successor or Co-Founder |
| **Architecture documentation** | Updated system architecture document, deployment runbooks |
| **Code walkthrough** | Minimum 20 hours of recorded knowledge-transfer sessions |
| **Ongoing commitments** | List of open PRs, pending migrations, known technical debt |
| **Third-party accounts** | Transfer ownership of all vendor/service accounts (Azure, GitHub, domain registrar, etc.) |

#### Commercial Co-Founder Handover
| Item | Deliverable |
|---|---|
| **Client relationships** | Warm introduction of all active clients to successor or Technical Founder |
| **Pipeline** | Full CRM export with status, next steps, and contact details for every opportunity |
| **Partner agreements** | Transition of all partner/reseller relationships with introduction calls |
| **Sales materials** | All pitch decks, proposals, pricing negotiations documented |
| **Financial commitments** | List of all outstanding quotes, pending invoices, contractual obligations to clients |

**Failure to complete handover** within **30 days of the end of the notice period** constitutes grounds for Aggravated Bad Leaver treatment (Section 11.2.1).

### 11.6 Cause for Termination

Either founder may be removed as Geschäftsführer for cause (OR Art. 815), including:
- Proven fraud or embezzlement
- Conviction of a criminal offence related to business activities
- Wilful breach of fiduciary duty
- Persistent failure to perform agreed duties after written warning
- Competing activity in breach of Section 8

### 11.7 Bad Leaver Determination Process

To prevent disputes, the following formal process applies when classifying a founder as a Bad Leaver:

| Step | Action | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Written notice** | The accusing founder sends a **formal written notice** (Einschreiben) citing the specific Bad Leaver trigger and supporting evidence | Day 0 |
| **2. Response period** | The accused founder has **14 calendar days** to respond in writing, providing their explanation and any counter-evidence | Day 1–14 |
| **3. Mediation attempt** | If disputed, both founders engage a neutral Swiss mediator for a single session (max 1 day) | Day 15–30 |
| **4. Advisory Board review** | If mediation fails and an Advisory Board exists, the Advisory Board issues a **binding recommendation** within 14 days | Day 30–44 |
| **5. Arbitration** | If no Advisory Board exists or the recommendation is rejected, the matter goes to **expedited arbitration** (Swiss Chambers, Zurich, single arbitrator) | Day 45–90 |
| **6. Share escrow** | From Day 0 (notice), the accused founder's **unvested shares are frozen** — no transfer, no voting rights on those shares — until the process concludes | Duration of process |

- **Burden of proof** lies with the **accusing party** for all Bad Leaver triggers. For abandonment, the burden shifts to the **accused** to prove a valid reason for absence.
- During the determination process, both founders continue their duties (unless the accused founder's access poses a security risk, in which case garden leave applies).
- Costs of mediation and arbitration are borne by the **losing party**. If the complaint is found to be vexatious, the accusing party also pays reasonable legal fees of the accused.
- **Anti-abuse:** A founder who initiates a Bad Leaver process **more than twice without success** within any 12-month period is deemed to be acting in bad faith, which may itself constitute grounds for deadlock resolution (Section 10).

### 11.8 Share Repurchase Mechanics

When shares must be repurchased under Bad Leaver or Good Leaver provisions:

| Term | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| **Valuation date** | The date the Bad Leaver / Good Leaver notice is sent |
| **Valuation method (FMV)** | Most recent funding round price (if <12 months), otherwise Practitioner Method (KS Nr. 28) by independent auditor |
| **Payment** | Lump sum within **90 days** of final determination; or, if cash-constrained, up to **12 monthly instalments** with 3% p.a. interest |
| **Share transfer** | Departing founder must execute a **Zession** (share transfer agreement) within 10 days of receiving payment; company may withhold final payment until transfer is registered |
| **Tax** | Each party bears their own tax consequences. Company provides a written statement of repurchase price for the departing founder's tax return |
| **Escrow (if disputed)** | Repurchase funds deposited into a **joint escrow account** managed by the company's Treuhand until transfer is complete |

## 17. Founder Holding Structures & Tax Optimisation

### 17.1 Personal Holding Company (Recommended)

Each founder is **strongly recommended** to hold their ConTigo shares through a personal holding GmbH:

```
[Technical Founder] → owns 100% of → [TF Holding GmbH] → owns 70% of → [ConTigo GmbH]
[Co-Founder]        → owns 100% of → [CF Holding GmbH] → owns 25% of → [ConTigo GmbH]
```

**Tax advantages:**

| Benefit | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Capital gains on exit** | Sale of shares by individual = tax-free (Kapitalgewinnsteuer). Sale via holding = Beteiligungsabzug (participation deduction) reduces effective tax to near-zero |
| **Dividend tax optimisation** | Dividends from ConTigo to Holding: 95%+ exempt (Beteiligungsabzug). From Holding to individual: taxed at reduced rate (cantonal rules vary; ZH ~60% inclusion) |
| **Retained earnings** | Cash from exit stays in holding company; can be reinvested tax-efficiently |
| **Liability separation** | ConTigo liability doesn't flow to personal assets beyond holding |
| **Cost** | ~CHF 3,000–5,000 to set up; ~CHF 2,000/year for bookkeeping |

> **Timing:** Set up holding companies **before** GmbH incorporation to avoid triggering a taxable event on later restructuring.

### 17.2 Intellectual Property Holding (Optional, Post-Series A)

For larger scale, consider an IP holding structure:

```
[ConTigo IP GmbH] (Canton of Zug or Nidwalden — lower tax rate)
    │
    └── Licences IP to → [ConTigo GmbH] (Canton of Zurich — operating company)
```

**Tax advantage:** Patent box regime (STAF) allows up to 90% reduction on IP income in qualifying cantons. Effective corporate tax on IP income: ~5–10% vs ~20% in Zurich.

> **Note:** This structure is only cost-effective at >CHF 1M ARR. Requires careful transfer pricing documentation. Consult with tax advisor.

### 17.3 Wealth Tax Considerations

Swiss founders pay cantonal wealth tax on the value of their shares:

| Year | Valuation Method | Estimated Share Value | Wealth Tax (Zurich) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Year 1–3 | Nominal value (Kreisschreiben Nr. 28) | CHF 1/share | Negligible |
| Year 3–5 | Practitioner method (weighted earnings + assets) | CHF 10–50/share | CHF 500–2,000/year |
| Post-Series A | Last round valuation | Per round price | Could be significant |

> **Tip:** Under Kreisschreiben Nr. 28, startup shares can be valued at nominal value for the first years if the company is pre-revenue or early-revenue. Document this with the Treuhänder.

## 18. Personal Circumstances & Force Majeure

### 18.1 Divorce / Separation

Swiss matrimonial property law (Güterrecht) can affect share ownership:

| Regime | Impact on Shares | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| **Errungenschaftsbeteiligung** (default) | Shares acquired during marriage may be subject to 50/50 division of increase in value | Prenuptial agreement (Ehevertrag) strongly recommended |
| **Gütertrennung** (separation of assets) | Shares remain with the owning spouse | Safest option; requires notarised Ehevertrag |
| **Gütergemeinschaft** (joint property) | All assets are joint | Not recommended for founders |

> **Recommendation:** Both founders should enter into a prenuptial agreement (Ehevertrag) or postnuptial amendment **before incorporation** that excludes company shares from marital property division. Cost: CHF 1,000–3,000 (notary).

#### 18.1.1 Spousal Consent & Acknowledgement

If either founder is married or in a registered partnership at the time of incorporation:

1. The founder’s spouse/partner must sign a **Spousal Acknowledgement Letter** confirming:
   - They are aware of this Founders’ Agreement and the equity ownership structure
   - They acknowledge the company shares are the founder’s separate professional asset
   - They will not assert a claim on the shares in the event of divorce/separation beyond what is required by mandatory Swiss law
   - They consent to the transfer restrictions (Vinkulierung) and Bad Leaver provisions that may result in share repurchase
2. If the founder marries or enters a registered partnership **after** incorporation, they must obtain the spousal acknowledgement within **90 days** and provide a copy to the other founder
3. Failure to obtain spousal acknowledgement does not invalidate this agreement but may be raised as a risk factor in the annual review (Section 19)

> **Template:** A Spousal Acknowledgement Letter template should be prepared by the company’s attorney and is attached as Schedule C.

### 18.2 Personal Insolvency / Bankruptcy

|---|---|---|
| Founder declared personally bankrupt (Konkurs) | Betreibungsamt may seize shares to satisfy creditors | Holding company structure (Section 17) isolates shares |
| Founder subject to debt enforcement (Pfändung) | Creditors may seize shares via Betreibungsamt | Vinkulierung (transfer restrictions in Articles) blocks forced sale to third parties |

### 20.1 Scope

All information exchanged between founders regarding the company, its technology, customers, finances, and strategy is **strictly confidential**. This includes:
- Source code and architecture
- Customer data and pipeline
- Financial projections and actuals
- Fundraising terms and investor relationships
- This Founders' Agreement itself

### 20.2 Duration

Confidentiality obligations survive termination of this agreement:
- **Trade secrets** (source code, AI architecture, prompt engineering, customer data): **perpetual** (until information becomes publicly available through no fault of the disclosing party)
- **Business information** (financials, strategy, pricing, pipeline): **7 years** from departure
- **This Agreement itself**: **5 years** from last amendment or company dissolution, whichever is later

### 20.3 Exceptions

Disclosure is permitted:
- To legal and financial advisors under professional privilege
- As required by Swiss law or court order
- To potential investors during due diligence (under NDA)
- With written consent of all founders

### 20.4 Penalties for Breach (Konventionalstrafe)

Breach of confidentiality obligations triggers:

| Breach Type | Penalty (OR Art. 160 ff.) | Additional |
|---|---|---|
| **Disclosure of trade secrets** | CHF 100,000 per incident | Plus actual damages |
| **Disclosure of customer data** | CHF 50,000 per incident | Plus regulatory exposure (nDSG/FADP fines up to CHF 250,000) |
| **Disclosure of financial information** | CHF 25,000 per incident | Plus actual damages |
| **Disparaging public statements** | CHF 25,000 per incident | Plus injunctive relief |

- Penalties are payable **without proof of actual damage** (Konventionalstrafe per OR Art. 160)
- Penalties do **not** replace the right to claim additional damages beyond the penalty amount (OR Art. 161 para. 2)
- The company may seek **provisional injunctive measures** (vorsorgliche Massnahmen) under ZPO Art. 261 ff. to prevent further disclosure

### 20.5 Return of Confidential Information

Upon departure for any reason, the departing founder must within **7 days**:
1. Return all physical documents, devices, access badges, and company property
2. Permanently delete all company data from personal devices, cloud storage, and email accounts
3. Revoke all personal access to company systems (or cooperate with the remaining founder to do so)
4. Sign a **Confidential Information Return Certificate** confirming compliance
5. Submit to a reasonable verification process (e.g., IT audit of personal devices, with privacy safeguards)
